# Model Selection Is Engineering

Choosing an AI model or tool is an engineering decision. The right choice depends on task risk, required context, feedback speed, cost, privacy, and the cost of being wrong.

For a small formatting pass, an inexpensive fast model may be enough. For a design review, migration plan, or subtle bug hunt, paying more for stronger reasoning can be cheaper than accepting shallow output and spending hours repairing it. Those are personal estimates and trade-offs, not audited invoices.

The useful habit is to make the choice explicit. Match the model to the work, constrain the task, verify the result, and change tools when the evidence says the current one is the wrong fit.
